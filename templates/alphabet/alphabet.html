Introduction to the Japanese Alphabet

What is the Japanese Alphabet?

The Japanese writing system consists of three alphabets: Hiragana, Katakana, and Kanji. Each alphabet serves a different purpose:

Hiragana (ひらがな)

Hiragana is used for native Japanese words, particles, and grammatical elements. It is the first alphabet learners typically study.

Learn Hiragana

Katakana (カタカナ)

Katakana is used for foreign words, names, and onomatopoeia. It is similar to Hiragana but has a distinct angular style.

Learn Katakana

Kanji (漢字)

Kanji are Chinese characters used in Japanese writing to represent whole words or concepts. There are thousands of Kanji characters, but about 2,000 are commonly used.

Learn kanji
